Competition Rules

The competition invites participation from individuals in both industry and academia, encompassing both students and professionals interested in making meaningful contributions to the field. The following outlines the provisional rules for our competition.

  • At most 10 participants per team from one or multiple affiliations; larger teams not allowed for fairness.
  • There will be no limit on the number of teams per institute/organization; no common individual would be allowed across teams from the same institute/ organization.
  • Each person would be able to participate on one team, including mentors, with no exceptions.
  • All registered team should develop models for the task and evaluate on the provided test set. All the uploaded results would have to confirm to a shared format.
  • Participants would be allowed to use publicly available training datasets or private data, with requirement to disclose details of private datasets.
  • The organization team will verify the format of the results. The leader board would automatically calculates metrics and displays the method,team name/ID, and scores in order.
  • Each team will have the chance to upload their results up to 10 times. However, the restriction on the number of submissions will be lifted once the competition ends.
  • To verify results, team members must upload technique descriptions and are encouraged to submit final trained models along with inference code (optional).
  • Winner and Runner up teams will have certificates listing the names of their members in the exact format and order as they have registered.
